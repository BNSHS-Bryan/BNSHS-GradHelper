University of the Philippines

University of the Philippines

The University of the Philippines (UP) is the country’s national university. This premier institution of higher learning was established in 1908 and is now a university system composed of eight constituent universities located in 17 campuses all over the archipelago. As of 2020, the Commission on Higher Education has declared 41 units of the university as Centers of Excellence; while 5 units have been declared as Centers of Development.

University of Santo Thomas

University of Santo Thomas

The University of Santo Tomas (UST) is a prestigious institution deeply rooted in Philippine history and culture. Founded in 1611, UST holds the distinction of being the oldest existing university in Asia. It has evolved into a comprehensive university system with campuses and colleges spread across different regions of the country. UST takes pride in its diverse academic programs and the recognition it has received from the Commission on Higher Education. With numerous Centers of Excellence and Centers of Development, UST is committed to providing quality education, fostering research, and shaping future leaders in various fields.

Ateneo de Manila University

Ateneo de Manila University

Ateneo de Manila University (ADMU) stands as a beacon of academic excellence and Ignatian values in the Philippines. Founded in 1859, ADMU has been at the forefront of providing holistic education that integrates intellectual rigor, social awareness, and spiritual formation. With a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across various disciplines, ADMU fosters a culture of critical thinking, creativity, and ethical leadership. The university's commitment to academic excellence has garnered recognition from various accrediting bodies, affirming its position as one of the country's premier educational institutions. At ADMU, students are empowered to become men and women for others, equipped to make a positive impact in society through their knowledge, skills, and values.
